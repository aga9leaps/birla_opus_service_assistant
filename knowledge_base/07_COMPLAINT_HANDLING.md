# Complaint Handling Guide

## Complaint Handling Principles

1. **Listen First** - Let customer explain fully
2. **Acknowledge** - Show you understand their frustration
3. **Investigate** - Don't assume, check facts
4. **Resolve** - Find solution, not excuses
5. **Follow-up** - Ensure customer is satisfied

---

## Common Complaints & Resolution Scripts

### Complaint 1: "Paint is peeling after 2-3 months"

**Step 1: Acknowledge**
> "I understand this is frustrating. Paint shouldn't peel so soon. Let me help you resolve this."

**Step 2: Gather Information**
- When was painting completed?
- Which rooms/areas are affected?
- Is there any water leakage or dampness nearby?
- Was waterproofing done before painting?
- Do you have photos?

**Step 3: Site Visit (within 48 hours)**
- Take photos of affected areas
- Check moisture levels with meter
- Check if proper process was followed
- Identify root cause

**Step 4: Resolution Based on Cause**

| Root Cause | Action | Cost to Customer |
|------------|--------|------------------|
| Workmanship defect (improper prep) | Full repaint of affected area | FREE (warranty) |
| Hidden dampness discovered | Fix water source + waterproof + repaint | Customer pays for WP |
| Structural crack causing issue | Explain, quote for proper repair | Customer pays |
| Customer damage (nail holes, etc.) | Quote for touch-up | Customer pays |

**Step 5: Follow-up**
- Call after 7 days
- Ensure issue is fully resolved
- Document in service record

---

### Complaint 2: "Color doesn't match what I selected"

**Step 1: Acknowledge**
> "I understand colors are important to you. Let's check what happened."

**Step 2: Investigate**
- Check color code on paint can vs quote
- Check if same batch was used
- Natural light vs artificial light appearance
- Wall condition affecting color perception

**Step 3: Common Explanations**
- Colors look different under different lighting
- Screen colors vs actual paint differ
- Wet paint looks different than dry paint
- Multiple coats needed for true color

**Step 4: Resolution**

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Wrong color code applied | Repaint with correct color - FREE |
| Color matches code but customer doesn't like | Offer to repaint at cost (material + labor) |
| Lighting causing perception difference | Show color in different lighting, educate |

---

### Complaint 3: "Work is taking too long"

**Step 1: Acknowledge**
> "I understand you want this completed quickly. Let me check on the progress."

**Step 2: Check Status**
- What was original timeline?
- What's causing delay?
- How much work remains?

**Step 3: Common Delay Reasons**
- Weather (can't paint in rain/extreme heat)
- Additional work discovered (hidden dampness, cracks)
- Material delivery delay
- Painter availability

**Step 4: Resolution**
- Provide updated realistic timeline
- Explain delay reason honestly
- Add extra painter if possible
- Offer daily progress updates

---

### Complaint 4: "Painters made mess / damaged property"

**Step 1: Acknowledge**
> "I'm very sorry about this. Our painters should be careful with your property."

**Step 2: Assess Damage**
- What exactly was damaged?
- Take photos
- Get repair estimate if needed

**Step 3: Resolution**

| Damage Type | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| Paint spots on floor/furniture | Clean immediately, use appropriate solvent |
| Minor scratches | Touch up or repair at our cost |
| Significant damage | Get repair quote, compensate customer |

**Step 4: Prevention**
- Remind painters about proper covering
- Document for painter performance review

---

### Complaint 5: "Price seems too high"

**Step 1: Don't Be Defensive**
> "I understand you want value for money. Let me explain the quote."

**Step 2: Explain Value**
- Break down quote components (material, labor, service)
- Explain quality of Birla Opus products
- Highlight warranty coverage
- Compare with long-term value (not cheapest)

**Step 3: Options**
- Suggest economy products (Style range) if budget is concern
- Offer to reduce scope (fewer rooms now)
- Check for any current promotions

**Step 4: Never Compromise On**
- Product quality (always Birla Opus)
- Proper process (no skipping steps)
- Safety measures

---

### Complaint 6: "Painter didn't show up / came late"

**Step 1: Apologize**
> "I sincerely apologize for the inconvenience. This shouldn't happen."

**Step 2: Immediate Action**
- Contact painter immediately
- Arrange alternative painter if needed
- Confirm new time with customer

**Step 3: Follow-up**
- Ensure painter arrives as promised
- Small gesture of goodwill if significant inconvenience

**Step 4: Internal Action**
- Record against painter's profile
- Address with painter for repeat issues

## Warranty Claims Process

### What's Covered (Workmanship Warranty - 1 Year)
- Peeling/flaking due to application defect
- Uneven finish due to improper technique
- Color inconsistency within same job
- Drips/sags not corrected during job

### What's NOT Covered
- Structural defects (building cracks, settlement)
- Water damage from leakage
- Customer-caused damage
- Normal wear and tear
- Fading from sun exposure (beyond product warranty)
- Issues due to customer not following care instructions

### Claim Process
1. Customer contacts dealer with complaint
2. Dealer visits site within 48 hours
3. Dealer assesses and documents issue
4. If valid warranty claim:
   - Schedule repair/repaint
   - Complete within 7 days
   - No charge to customer
5. If not covered:
   - Explain reason to customer
   - Offer paid repair option
   - Provide quote if customer interested

## Documentation Requirements

### For Every Complaint
1. Date and time of complaint
2. Customer details
3. Nature of complaint
4. Photos (before/after)
5. Root cause identified
6. Resolution provided
7. Customer sign-off
8. Follow-up date and outcome

### For Warranty Claims
Additional:
1. Original job date
2. Original quote/invoice
3. Products used
4. Painter who did the job
5. Claim assessment report

## Difficult Customer Situations

### Customer is Angry/Shouting
- Stay calm, don't argue
- Let them vent
- Acknowledge their frustration
- Focus on solution
- If abusive, politely end call and escalate

### Customer Threatening Bad Review/Legal Action
- Don't make promises you can't keep
- Document everything
- Escalate to dealer/Birla Opus immediately
- Focus on reasonable resolution

### Customer Demands Impossible Resolution
- Explain what's possible and why
- Offer best alternative
- Document their demand and your response
- Escalate if needed

---

## Service Recovery Tips

### Turn Complaint into Opportunity
- Resolve faster than expected
- Go slightly above what's required
- Personal call from dealer (not just painter)
- Small gesture (free touch-up of another room, discount on next service)

### Build Long-term Relationship
- "We'd love to make this right and earn your trust for future projects"
- Offer loyalty discount for next service
- Ask for referral once satisfied

---

## Complaint Prevention

### Before Job Starts
- Clear, written scope of work
- Realistic timeline communicated
- Proper covering of customer property
- Daily update commitment

### During Job
- Daily progress photos
- Flag issues immediately
- No surprises at the end
- Clean work site daily

### After Job Completion
- Thorough walkthrough with customer
- Address concerns before final payment
- Provide care instructions
- Follow-up call in 3 days
